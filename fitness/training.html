Training

I would say for the past 12 years I have trained pretty regularly. My training pattern has changed throughout this period where there has been times I would focus on lifting weights in the gym or I would priorities going out running over anything else. I always struggled to find the balance between CV and weight training as I would tend to favour one or the other, this would have a negative impact on the method of training I was steering away from.

More recently over the past couple of years and increasingly through lockdown I think I am in a good space. I have that balance of CV and resistance training, I am also doing a lot more calisthenics. Training for me now is a habit, it is something I know should be doing every day. I do this because I like the benefits. I like being in good health, I like sleeping well and I like having energy.

There is always time to train

I often hear people say 'I don't have time to train', for some people this could be true, others not so much. My first question when people say this is 'how long do you spend watching the TV at night?'. If say the answer to this question is an hour, what you have here is time to train and not enough time to watch TV! This boils down to priorities.

Now I am not making out that I train every single day religiously. There are those days when you have had a long horrible day at work and you just can't be bothered. I wouldn’t fault anyone for sacking training off on days like these, I have done it and I will do it again. But, what I have also done is train on these days too. Even if it is just a 20 minute after a stressful day it will make you feel better.

Have a plan you will stick to

I touch on this in the Ironman page. You need to have a plan which you can stick to. You could have the best training plan ever created but if you don’t stick to it it's pointless. I once asked a PT to draw me up a training plan, he just stuck a load of exercises down on a piece of paper, he didn't even ask if I had any injuries or what kind of training I enjoyed doing.

To me it is important to have a plan which I like, If I like it the chances are that I will stick to it. If you don’t like it you don’t have to do it. If you don’t like to run jump on a bike, if you don’t like to bike get on the rower.
